## What You Just Experienced

The **Flanker Task** is one of the most important tests of **selective attention** - your brain's ability to focus on relevant information while ignoring distractions. You likely noticed:

- **Easy trials** (congruent): When all arrows pointed the same way ’ ’ ’ ’ ’
- **Hard trials** (incongruent): When the center arrow differed   ’    
- **Medium trials** (neutral): When flankers were neutral   ’  

This is called the **"Flanker Effect"** - incongruent trials are typically slower and more error-prone because the flanking arrows create conflicting information that your brain must override.
